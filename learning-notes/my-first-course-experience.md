## What I'm Learning Today
I'm discovering that Cursor has two powerful features: Skills and Rules. 

**Skills** act like expert consultants I can call upon for specific tasks - writing assistance, content organization, or specialized help.

**Rules** work automatically in the background, gently guiding me toward good practices as I type.

The key insight: I don't need programming knowledge to benefit from these AI features!

## First Impressions
This feels like having a helpful teacher and expert assistants available whenever I need them. The combination of automatic guidance (Rules) and on-demand help (Skills) seems incredibly powerful for learning and organizing knowledge.

## Questions I Have
- **Skill Discovery**: How do I know when a Skill is being used? Is there visual feedback?
- **Rule Scope**: Will Rules work with any type of file I create, or just markdown?
- **Rule Flexibility**: What happens if I don't follow a Rule's suggestion? Can I ignore them?
- **Learning Path**: Which should I focus on first - mastering Skills or understanding Rules?
