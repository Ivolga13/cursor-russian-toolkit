# Learning Glossary

## Cursor Terms

**Agent**
The AI assistant that helps you with tasks in Cursor. It can read your files, write code, and use Skills.

**Rules**
Automatic guidelines that help maintain consistency in your work. They check your files and suggest improvements as you type.

**Skills** 
Specialized AI capabilities that help with specific tasks. Think of them as expert consultants you can call upon when needed.

**Workspace**
Your project folder where all your files, Skills, and Rules are organized.

## File and Organization Terms

**Markdown (.md)**
A simple formatting language that makes plain text look good. Uses symbols like # for headings and * for emphasis.

**Frontmatter**
Special information at the top of files, written between --- lines. Contains metadata like titles and descriptions.

**Glob Pattern**
A way to specify which files Rules should apply to. For example, `**/*.md` means "all markdown files in any folder."

## Writing and Documentation Terms

**Active Voice**
Writing style where the subject does the action. "I learned" instead of "It was learned by me."

**Progressive Disclosure**
Organizing information so basic details come first, with more complex details available when needed.

**Cross-Reference**
Links between related documents or sections. Helps readers find connected information.

## Learning Terms

**Knowledge Base**
A organized collection of information and notes. Your personal library of what you've learned.

**Learning in Public**
Sharing your learning process openly. Helps reinforce knowledge and helps others learn too.

**Reflection**
Thinking about what you've learned and how it connects to other knowledge. Essential for deep learning.

## Technical Terms (Simplified)

**Directory/Folder**
A container for organizing files. Like a filing cabinet drawer.

**File Path**
The address of a file, showing which folders contain it. Like a street address for files.

**Relative Path**
A file address that starts from your current location, not from the computer's root.

**Template**
A pre-made format or structure that you can copy and customize for your own use.

## Process Terms

**Iteration**
Repeating a process to improve it. Making something better through multiple rounds of changes.

**Workflow**
A series of steps you follow to complete a task efficiently and consistently.

**Best Practice**
A method or technique that has proven to work well and is recommended by experienced users.
